Data Requirements

Introduction:
Our application aims to streamline course management for students, providing features such as course history tracking, degree requirements completion status, and optional course recommendations based on user preferences and past courses. The following data requirement analysis outlines the key data categories necessary for the successful implementation of the application.

Course History Data:
One of the core use cases of our Course Tracker application revolves around managing a student's course history. This includes storing information about each course taken, such as the course name, semester, year, professor, credits earned, grades (optional), and any additional notes. As users interact with the application, adding and modifying their course history, the system must maintain accurate and organized records to facilitate functions like viewing past courses, tracking progress, and generating degree completion status reports using the course history data. Hence, the course history data is managed and displayed in the form of semester-segmented tables with each section containing the courses taken, the credits earned for each, and the total credits for that semester.

Degree Completion Status Data:
Another crucial aspect of our application is monitoring the completion status of degree requirements. This requires storing data related to the specific requirements for a particular degree program, such as mandatory courses, elective courses, credit thresholds, and completion criteria for different academic areas (e.g., mathematics, computer science core courses). Additionally, the system needs to track which requirements have been fulfilled by the user's completed courses, displaying progress indicators and highlighting completed sections for the user's reference on the Degree Completion Status page.

Course Recommendation Data:
To further enhance the functionality of our application, we are conceptualizing the potential and optional idea of incorporating a course recommendation feature based on various factors, including user interests, past course history, remaining semesters, and graduation requirements. To enable this functionality, the system requires data related to course offerings, prerequisites, course descriptions, and relevant academic pathways. Additionally, user preferences and historical data about courses taken by similar users may help build the recommendation algorithm.

User Account Information Data:
Our application will also have a user account page for managing personal information and preferences. This includes storing data such as the user's name, date of birth (if needed), anticipated semester of graduation, major, and any other relevant profile details. Additionally, our application may store authentication credentials for user login purposes, ensuring secure access to personalized features and data. While maintaining user privacy and security, this data enables personalized interactions with the application.

Conclusion:
To summarize, our Course Tracker application requires rigorous data management to support its core functionalities effectively. From maintaining comprehensive course histories to tracking degree completion progress and offering personalized recommendations, the system must handle diverse data types efficiently in order to provide students with valuable insights, streamline academic planning, and enhance their overall educational experience.

Wireframes

Course History Page



This wireframe represents the central hub for students to visualize their academic progress over time. By showcasing a carousel of past semesters, ordered from latest to oldest, users can easily navigate navigate through their course history. Each semester section displays course details such as name, credits earned, and a cumulative total in tabular form. Course Completion Status Page



This wireframe offers students a comprehensive overview of their progress towards degree completion. Through categorized sections highlighting the diverse academic areas needed for graduation, users can identify their course requirements. A subtle visual cue, such as a green tick against completed courses, enhances clarity. Dynamic progress bars provide real-time feedback, ensuring users remain informed about their degree progress.

Adding a Course Page



This wireframe presents a straightforward interface for students to seamlessly add new courses to their tracker. The form's structured layout, including mandatory fields for course details and optional sections for additional information, ensures users can input data efficiently. Real-World Connection

The motivation for the project comes from our personal experiences when it comes to course planning. Every semester, students choose the courses they are going to take the following semester. This process often includes first looking at all the courses they have taken in the past, checking the requirements they need to graduate, and then figuring out which courses they can take to fulfill those requirements. This process can be time-consuming and confusing, especially for students who have taken a lot of courses.

All students use some kind of a tracking sheet


Every student maintains some kind of a spreadsheet or document to keep track of this information. These sheet have some way to indicate total credits taken and progress towards graduation. Spire, our university tool, also include an ARR (Academic Requirements Report), but this report can be slow, hard to read, and sometimes inaccurate.
Our application aims to simplify this process by providing a single platform where students can save all the courses they have taken, the requirements they have fulfilled, and the requirements they still need to fulfill. This will help students plan their courses more efficiently and ensure they are on track to graduate on time.
